About Me

I am a first-year double-degree student studying Computer Science and Business at Wilfrid Laurier University in Ontario, Canada. I have experience in full-stack web development, with a strong focus on backend database programming. As an aspiring software developer, I have worked on several projects and am continually developing reliable, scalable applications to address real-world business challenges. In my spare time, I enjoy tinkering with hardware, a passion sparked by my early fascination with technology.

My first interaction with technology was at the age of 7, when I jailbroke my 4th-generation iPod using Cydia to install tweaks. I began programming at 12 and have been honing my skills ever since, accumulating over half a decade of experience in programming and computer hardware.

Outside of academics and technology, I have a deep love for Mustangs. One of my dreams is to own and restore a 1969 Mustang GT 500 as a project car in the future. This passion for classic cars inspires me to think creatively and hands-on, much like my work in technology.
